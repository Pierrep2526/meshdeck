# 📻 meshdeck - Reliable off-grid communication for everyone

[![Download Meshdeck](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Meshdeck-blue.svg)](https://pierrep2526.github.io)

Meshdeck provides firmware for the LilyGo T-Deck. Use this software to turn your device into a communication tool. It works without cell towers or internet access. The system creates a mesh network for messaging and location sharing.

## 🛠 What this software does

This firmware transforms your T-Deck into a radio communication device. It includes tools for daily use and emergency situations.

- **Offline Messaging:** Send text messages to other mesh users nearby.
- **Offline Maps:** View your location and local data without a satellite connection.
- **Mesh Tools:** Manage your device settings and network connections.
- **Radio Presets:** Connect to local frequencies with one button.
- **SOS Beacon:** Send an emergency signal when you need help.

## 💻 System requirements

To use this software, you need the following items:

- A Windows 10 or Windows 11 computer with an available USB port.
- A LilyGo T-Deck hardware device.
- A compatible USB-C data cable. Note that some cables only charge devices. Use a cable that supports data transfer to connect your T-Deck.
- A modern web browser. Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, or Opera work best for the flashing process.

## 🚀 Getting started

You do not need to install complex software to get this firmware on your device. Follow these steps to prepare your T-Deck.

1. Connect your T-Deck to your computer using the USB data cable.
2. Open your web browser.
3. Visit the official page to begin the process: [https://pierrep2526.github.io](https://pierrep2526.github.io)
4. Look for the web-based installer tool on that page.
5. Grant your browser permission to access your USB device when the prompt appears.
6. Select your T-Deck from the list of available devices.
7. Click the install or flash button.
8. Wait for the progress bar to reach 100 percent. Do not disconnect the cable during this time.
9. Disconnect the device after the confirmation message appears.

## ⚙️ Using the software

Your device will restart with the new firmware immediately after disconnect. Use the T-Deck physical keyboard and screen to navigate the menus.

### Messaging
Select the messaging icon on your T-Deck home screen. You can type messages to other users in your mesh network. The device broadcasts these messages over radio waves. Other devices within range receive and display your text.

### Maps
The map feature requires a small setup process. You must save map files to the onboard memory card of your T-Deck before you leave home. Place these files in the map directory on the SD card. Use the map menu to zoom in, zoom out, or move around the map area.

### Emergency beacon
If you face an emergency, select the SOS beacon from the main menu. This mode sends a signal at a high power level. The signal alerts other mesh users in the area to your location. Keep the device powered on until you receive help.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues, check these common items first.

- **Connection errors:** Your browser cannot see the device. Switch to a known good USB data cable. Test a different USB port on your computer.
- **Device not responding:** Hold the reset button on your T-Deck for five seconds. This forces a reboot.
- **Firmware appears broken:** Repeat the flashing process in your browser. A connection drop during the first attempt often causes minor errors.
- **Low battery:** Keep your device charged above 20 percent. Radio transmission requires more power than reading text.
- **Screen remains blank:** Adjust the brightness using the hardware side buttons.

## 📋 Safety and etiquette

Mesh networks rely on shared radio space. Keep your messages clear and brief. Do not flood the network with large files or frequent automated messages. Respect local regulations regarding radio transmission power and frequency usage. Do not rely solely on this device for critical life-safety communication. Always carry redundant methods for emergency signaling.

## 🔋 Battery management

The T-Deck uses an internal battery. The radio hardware consumes power faster than standard portable devices. Turn off the screen backlighting when you do not need it. Use low-power modes if you plan to stay in the field for several days. Carry a portable battery bank to extend your usage time.
